About me

I’m a recent graduate of the University of California, Berkeley where I studied Electrical Engineering and Computer Science. I’ve taken courses in Databases, Machine Learning, Algorithms, Data Structures, Optimization, Probability and Random Processes, and Data Science and have supplemented this coursework with higher mathematics courses such as linear algebra, abstract algebra, real analysis and cryptography. I’ve always been drawn to analytical thinking and mathematics which has pushed me to focus on building foundational understanding of statistics, algorithms, machine learning, and algorithms while in college. While at Berkeley, I’ve been able to tutor fellow CS students by participating in Computer Science Mentors (CSM) at Berkeley and by holding positions such as Tutor and Academic Intern for required technical classes such as CS70: Discrete Mathematics and Probability Theory and EE16A: Designing Information Devices and Systems.

In conjunction with my academic pursuits, as my technical expertise and mastery of particular programming languages/libraries have grown I’ve been able to develop side projects ranging from the statistical analysis of online resale markets to building a Course Recommender powered by historical academic data. When my time isn’t being spent on anything CS-related, I avidly enjoy weightlifting and also playing chess ("chesschess8" on http://chess.com). Although, these activities don’t always feel taking a break!

I’m looking forward to building a career in software and I am actively pursuing employment opportunities! Feel to reach out to me through email or LinkedIn.
